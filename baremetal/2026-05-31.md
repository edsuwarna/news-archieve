## 📊 Bare Metal Trend Summary

- **GPU Bare-Metal Boom is Full Throttle** — Lambda's $1B credit facility, ASUS's 8× B300 server, and the HGX B200 STAC-AI benchmarks all point to an AI infrastructure arms race playing out in physical hardware. Bare-metal GPU leasing is the new growth frontier.
- **BMaaS Goes Mainstream** — Lambda's "Bare Metal Instances" launch with BlueField DPUs and full API automation signals that cloud-like flexibility on physical servers is no longer niche. Expect AWS, Azure, and GCP to respond with more bare-metal SKUs.
- **Arm in the Datacenter is Real** — The Xsight Labs E1 running vanilla Ubuntu proves Arm DPUs are deployable today. Combined with AMD Zen 6 on the horizon, the x86 duopoly faces genuine competition in physical infrastructure.
- **Hardware Strategy is Splintering** — Equinix's "Agentic AI" thesis says one-size-fits-all GPU clusters won't cut it. Bare-metal operators need to plan for diverse hardware profiles: inference-optimized, training-density, edge-constrained, and finance-grade deterministic.
- **Storage and Networking Keep Innovating** — PCIe Gen5 DRAM-less SSD controllers (Silicon Motion) and 10GbE+ NAS options (ZimaCube) show that the surrounding hardware ecosystem continues to push density and efficiency, even outside the GPU spotlight.
